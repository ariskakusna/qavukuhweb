Real estate Sydney: Home sells $7m above price guide

Publish date: 2024-08-15

The luxury home of a banking boss has sold for an extraordinary price, leaving the sales agent shocked.

Oliver Lavers of The Rubinstein Group initially had a $13.8m guide for 5 Fairweather St, Bellevue Hill, lifted to $14m to $14.5m guide in recent weeks as interest grew.

But at 9pm last night he sold it for $21m, with four parties fighting over it. It had five bedrooms and a pool.

“We had a strong offer come in at $17m and we had four competing up to $19m, three to $20m and two up to $21m,” he said.

The property, due to go under the hammer on November 4, was the home of The Australia Chief Executive Officer of Bank of America, Joe Fayyad, and his wife, Fiona, who’d bought it for $9.26m in 2021.

They’d only just completed a renovation by highly regarded architect Tony Freeman, with renowned designer Cameron Kimber doing the interiors and Pepo Botanic Design doing the landscaping.

Lavers had based his original price guide on his $15.2m sale of 10 Fairweather St across the road, which had eight buyers fighting for it at a June auction.

“We thought we had a chance of getting to that, but we weren’t sure,” Lavers said.

It had set a street record at the time.

The No.1 sales agent for the Ray White Group for the NSW and ACT said it just goes to show the power of a good reno in the current market. The vendors had spent more than $4m on it.

“Well-finished homes of this quality are the ones that are selling the strongest, because the cost of renovating has gone up so much,” Lavers said.

The buyers were a family from Double Bay. All four contenders were locals.

The home was previously owned by William O’Dwyer, the bankrupt founder of Ralan Group, which was one of the country’s biggest private developers before it went under, leaving apartment buyers about $230m out of pocket.
