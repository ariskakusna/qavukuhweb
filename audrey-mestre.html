Audrey Mestre (born 11th August 1974, age: 28 when died in 2002) was a popular world record free-diver from France. She was known for setting the record of 130 meters (427 feet) dive in one single breath. She was popular among sports stars during the era of the 90s. But her passion for free-diving took her life on October 12, 2022 when she was setting another world record in free-diving.

And after her death many controversies were raised and many documentaries were released. Her husband Francisco Rodriguez was near to her at the time of her death. He faced a lot of criticism after Audrey’s death.

Audrey Mestre Wiki, Biography (Early Life & Education)

Stating all the information about Audrey Mestre’s early life then she was born in Saint-Denis, France. Her birthdate was August 11, 1974. She roses her passion for freediving from her grandfather with whom she use to go on diving South of France. Her grandfather usually took her to swimming classes during winter and also in summer.

She was only 2 and a half years old when she competed in the 25-meter swimming competition. She won this competition and brought her life’s first gold medal. She was having big feet due to which she gets benefited while swimming.

When Audrey Mestre reached her 13th year of age she tried her hand at scuba diving. She later got her first certification in diving at the age of 16. When she was 16 she shifted with her family to Mexico and ended her high schooling there in 1993. Her passion for sea diving and swimming let her choose to study marine biology. Audrey got her degree in Marine biology from La Paz University.

Audrey Mestre Husband, Boyfriend & Relationships

The life of Audrey Mestre got changed when she married Francisco Ferreras “Francisco Pipin Ferreras” on August 18th, 1999. Before getting married to Pipin Audrey became a fan of her because he was also a world champion free-diver. Audrey researched and studied a lot about Pipin from newspapers, magazines, and from other sources. Then in February 1996, she got the chance to meet Pipin when he organized a freediving world record attempt in Cabo San Lucas, Baja California, Mexico.

There to get connected with Pipin Audrey started training under him and got the chance to talk with him. She has a long conversation with him. Later Pipin invited him for a dive. Then Pipin saw Audrey’s skill as a diver and got impressed.

Since then Audrey Mestre has begun to live with Pipin and usually helps him in training work. Audrey set her first world record under the guidance and supervision of Pipin. And later they both got married in 1999. They both were living in Miami, Florida.

Career- Free Diver (Final Dive Video, Book)

Audrey Mestre began her professional career after getting a lot of training from her husband Francsico Ferreras.  In 1997 Audrey began to set an underwater free-diving world record. However, she got succeeded in her attempt in 1999. In 1999 she broke the female world record in free-diving. She set a new record of 125 meters (410 ft) dive in one breath. Then in 2000, she set another world record-breaking her own record by diving 130 meters deep in one breath.

How did Audrey Mestre Die in 2002? (Documentary, Cause of Death)

It was the year 2002 a record of Audrey Mestre of 130 meters free dive was broken by a professional diver Tanya Streeter. Tanya set the record for 160-meter dive. Audrey made her attempt to break the 160-meter record.

On October 12, 2002, she began to break the record for 160-meter dive. Guess what Audrey actually reached 171 meters in her attempt. But destiny decided something else for her. When she reached 171 meters she tried to open the valve of the air tank to inflate the lift bag so that she could reach the surface at a high speed. But Audrey Mestre found that her cylinder is empty and had no air in it.

The rescue diver reached her to help and brought her out of the but it was too late. It took 8 and half minutes to bring Audrey out from the sea.

When she was brought to the surface no proper equipment to help her was there. No medical team was there. And when she was brought to the surface her pulse was working. But due to no medical team near her, it get too late to reach the hospital and she died.

Later her cremation ceremony was organized and her ashes were scattered in the sea.
